F.A.S.T. - Recognizing a Stroke

Time is critical when responding to a stroke. Remember the acronym F.A.S.T. to identify the signs and take immediate action.

Face

Has the person's face dropped or become uneven? Ask them to smile. Does one side of their face droop?

Arms

Can the person raise both arms equally? Ask them to hold their arms out straight for a few seconds. Does one arm drift downward?

Speech

Is the person's speech slurred or difficult to understand? Ask them to repeat a simple phrase. Is their speech garbled?

Time

If you observe any of these signs, call emergency services immediately. Every minute counts!

Remember: F.A.S.T. can help you recognize a stroke quickly. If you see any of these signs, call emergency services right away. Don't wait for the symptoms to pass - a stroke is a medical emergency.
