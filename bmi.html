Understanding BMI Numbers

Body Mass Index (BMI)

BMI is a tool used to estimate body fat based on height and weight. It's a simple calculation but doesn't directly measure body fat. However, it can be a helpful indicator of potential health risks associated with weight.

BMI Categories and Meanings

Here's a breakdown of BMI categories and their meanings according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC):

BMI Range Weight Status
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 to 24.9 Healthy Weight
25.0 to 29.9 Overweight
30.0 and Above Obese

**Important Note:** BMI is a general guideline. It may not be as accurate for people with:

  • Very muscular builds (muscle weighs more than fat)
  • Older adults (muscle mass may decrease with age)
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women

If you have concerns about your weight or BMI, talk to your doctor. They can consider your BMI along with other factors for a more personalized assessment of your health.
